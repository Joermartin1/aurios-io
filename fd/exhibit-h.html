1 — Media Digitization & Preservation

The ingestion stations. Where stranded assets become queryable memory. Priority category for Auri's — this is what most first-time members will walk in with.

High-speed sheet-fed scanner with ADF and duplex (Fujitsu ScanSnap, Epson DS-series)
Large format flatbed scanner — A3/A2 capable (Epson Perfection V850)
Overhead book scanner or DIY scanning rig
Dedicated film/slide scanner (Plustek OpticFilm, Pacific Image)
Negative carriers and film holders
High-resolution digital camera setup for large format scanning
VHS / Betamax / Hi8 / MiniDV capture devices
Video capture cards (Blackmagic, Elgato)
Time-base correctors for legacy formats
Cassette deck with USB output
Turntable with preamp (vinyl digitization)
Reel-to-reel player
Audio interface (Focusrite, PreSonus)
Floppy disk drives — 3.5" and 5.25"
Multi-format card readers — SD, CF, microSD, XQD
External Blu-ray / DVD writer
High-capacity NAS for temporary ingestion storage
UV-proof containers for resin / media storage
Filament dry boxes (repurposed for media storage)

2 — Computer & Electronics Repair

The data recovery stations. Hard drives, dead computers, forensic imaging.

Drive docking stations — USB 3.0, SATA/IDE multi-bay
Write blockers for forensic imaging
Cloning software licenses (Clonezilla, Macrium Reflect)
Drive enclosures — 2.5", 3.5", M.2, NVMe
Data recovery software (R-Studio, EaseUS, DMDE)
Disk imaging duplicator hardware
Precision screwdriver sets (iFixit toolkit)
Anti-static mats and wrist straps
Thermal paste and cleaning supplies
Cable testers — Ethernet, USB, HDMI
POST diagnostic cards
Spare common parts — RAM, CMOS batteries, SATA cables
Drive adapters — IDE, SCSI to modern
Basic dust-free enclosure for minor drive repair
⚑ True hard drive "head swap" clean room work is beyond the scope of a general maker lab. Focus should be on data recovery from logically or lightly physically damaged drives. Flag for specialist referral if platters are damaged.

911 Fisher St. specifics: 18-foot doors, overhead crane, ~7,000 sq ft interior, half-acre exterior. Climate control and power infrastructure are foundational — plan before equipment.

Notes & Recommendations

—Survey members first. Prioritize based on actual demand — media digitization, electronics repair, or prototyping. The Founding Circle is the first survey group.
—Start with gateway tools. High-speed scanner, good soldering station, quality multimeter — these serve the widest audience and have the lowest barrier to use. Every member who walks in benefits from these three.
—Training budget is not optional. Stock manuals and quick-start guides. Schedule workshops for soldering, oscilloscope use, and data recovery before opening those stations to unsupervised use.
—Insurance and liability. Check coverage for member use of power tools, electronics equipment, and chemical processes before opening.
—Maintenance budget. Consumables and calibration (soldering tips, multimeter calibration) add 10–15% annually. Budget for this from day one.
—The overhead crane and exterior yard are differentiators. Most maker spaces cannot accommodate anything larger than what fits through a standard door. Auri's can. Market this — it is the reason a solar pontoon boat could be the first showcase project.
—Phase the buildout. Start with Starter tier. Add Intermediate as membership grows. Advanced and Full Build come with demonstrated demand and revenue. Do not buy the table saw before the first ten members have asked for one.
