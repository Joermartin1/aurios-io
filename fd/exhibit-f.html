IP Concepts — Full Detail

IP-01 — PRIORITY

Query Preview™

Concept: Pre-submit query routing transparency with user override. As a user types in Ask Auri™, the system reads the query in real time, classifies it by type, and displays a suggested routing — which Partner AIs and memory sources are best suited — before the user submits. The user accepts with Enter or overrides with a tap.

Novel element: The specific combination of: (1) real-time pre-submit query classification, (2) routing suggestion display to the user before submission, and (3) user override capability before submission. No known production system provides routing transparency at the pre-submit stage with user control.

Prior art: Perplexity auto-routes queries but does so invisibly and without user control. ChatGPT has no routing. No system displays routing suggestions pre-submit with user override.

Prior art concern: Low — no known direct prior art on the specific combination.

Status: Concept stage. Not yet built.

Identified: May 2026, during Nomenclature Library development.

⚑ Flag for patent search and provisional application. Coordinate with IP-02 (Prompt Assist™) as they are closely related and may be filed together as the Ask Auri™ Pre-Submit Intelligence layer.
IP-02 — PRIORITY

Prompt Assist™

Concept: Memory-informed pre-submit prompt improvement. As the user types, Auri™ retrieves relevant personal memory and project context, then offers a rewritten version of the query before submission. Three layers: (1) substantive prompt rewrite informed by personal memory incorporating context the user has not yet typed, (2) routing optimization toward the best Partner AI, and (3) spelling and grammar correction as an inherent byproduct of the full redraft.

Novel element: Using retrieved personal memory to suggest a substantive prompt improvement before the query is submitted. The memory-informed substantive redraft at the pre-submit stage is the novel claim. Grammar and spelling improvement is a byproduct, not the core claim.

Prior art: Post-submit query rewriting exists in Perplexity and some RAG systems. Autocomplete exists broadly. Spell and grammar checking exists broadly.

Prior art concern: Low to moderate — post-submit rewriting has prior art but pre-submit memory-informed redraft appears clear.

Status: Concept stage. Not yet built. Highest novelty of all IP items identified to date.

Identified: May 2026, during Nomenclature Library development.

⚑ Flag as highest priority for provisional patent application. Consider filing IP-01 and IP-02 together as related claims.
IP-03 — HIGH

Auri™ Identity Interface (AII)

Concept: A user-controlled, AI-mediated API allowing external platforms to access verified personal preference and identity context from AuriOS™, with Member-defined disclosure levels per platform relationship. Example: Apple Music today infers listening preferences from streaming behavior on its platform only. With the AII, Apple Music queries AuriOS™ and receives verified context that the Member has approved for disclosure — producing a richer and more accurate signal than behavioral inference alone.

Novel element: The combination of: (1) AI mediation between Member identity and platform query — Auri™ translates the Member's curated identity into whatever schema the platform requests, (2) democratic governance of the identity data store via the Memory Entity, and (3) per-platform Member-defined disclosure control. No prior system combines all three elements.

Prior art: Solid (Tim Berners-Lee's personal data pod project), MyData initiative, and various personal data locker projects address portions of this concept. None combine the AI mediation layer with democratic governance and per-platform disclosure control.

Prior art concern: Moderate — Solid and MyData have been publicly documented for years. Claims must be scoped carefully to the AI mediation layer and governance combination.

Status: Concept stage. Named and defined.

Identified: May 2026, during Nomenclature Library development.

⚑ Flag for Legal Counsel review before any external development, publication, or partnership discussion. The AI mediation layer is the strongest novel claim — ensure claims are scoped to this specifically.
IP-04 — HIGH

AISP™ — AI Service Provider as Category Name

Concept: AI Service Provider (AISP™) as a defined product and market category for providers of personal AI infrastructure. The generational analog to the ISP of the 1990s. AuriOS™ is the first AISP™. The category name was coined by Joe R Martin.

Novel element: Category naming and first use of the specific defined term AISP™ in the context of personal AI infrastructure as a service. The term does not appear to be in active use as a defined category in AI industry literature as of May 2026.

Prior art: AISP™ as an acronym may exist in other contexts — financial services uses AISP™ for Account Information Service Provider in PSD2 regulation. The specific definition — provider of user-owned, locally-stored, democratically governed personal AI infrastructure — is distinct from all known prior uses.

Prior art concern: Moderate — acronym collision with PSD2 financial services term. Trademark search required before filing.

Status: Concept stage. Category coined and in active use in AuriOS™ documentation.

Identified: May 2026, during IBT/AuriOS™ development sessions.

⚑ Trademark search required immediately. Evaluate whether AISP™ conflicts with PSD2 AISP™ in trademark classes relevant to AuriOS™.
IP-05 — STANDARD

IBT Mode™ Synthesis

Concept: Sending a single prompt to multiple Partner AIs simultaneously, collecting all responses, and synthesizing them through an AI adjudication layer to produce a response no single model could produce alone. The synthesis layer evaluates, compares, ranks, and combines the outputs of multiple LLMs into a single coherent response with sourcing attribution.

Novel element: The multi-provider parallel query with AI-mediated synthesis as a consumer-facing product feature with user-visible attribution.

Prior art: Multi-LLM orchestration frameworks exist in enterprise settings — LangChain, LlamaIndex, and various agentic frameworks support routing queries to multiple models.

Prior art concern: Moderate — enterprise multi-LLM orchestration has prior art. Consumer-facing implementation with synthesis and attribution has less documented prior art.

Status: Implemented and working in Auri™ Console.

Identified: Prior to May 2026, during IBT development.

⚑ Flag for Legal Counsel review on specific synthesis methodology claims. The implemented system is prior art for AuriOS™'s own claims — document the implementation date and method in detail.
IP-06 — STANDARD

Vendor-Contextual AII Examples

Concept: The specific articulated examples of how the Auri™ Identity Interface (IP-03) operates with named commercial vendors (Apple Music, YouTube) as the querying party. The vendor-specific framing demonstrates a concrete use pattern: existing behavioral inference systems replaced by verified, Member-consented identity queries.

Novel element: The framing of verified personal AI identity as a commercial data product that replaces platform-owned behavioral inference, with the Member as the data principal rather than the data subject.

Prior art: Implicit in the AII concept. No independent prior art search conducted.

Prior art concern: Review alongside IP-03. Route to Legal Counsel.

Status: Concept stage. Documented in Founding Document and Nomenclature Library.

Identified: May 2026, during AII development.

IP-07 — HIGH

MQI™ Public Profile

Concept: A Member-controlled public-facing layer of AuriOS™ memory that, when queried by anyone, presents a personalized display — effectively a Personal Web Presence — without requiring the Member to maintain a domain name, hosting account, or separate platform presence. Updates automatically as the Member's AuriOS™ memory updates. Renders differently for different callers based on what the Member has authorized each category of visitor to see.

Novel element: The combination of: (1) AI-generated, always-current personal web presence derived from live personal memory, (2) PAIPy™ as the query mechanism replacing domain name resolution, (3) caller-differentiated rendering based on Member-defined disclosure levels, and (4) Memory Entity governance. No existing product combines all four elements.

Prior art: Personal data pods (Solid/Tim Berners-Lee) and MyData address data portability but not AI-mediated public presence rendering. Social profiles (LinkedIn, Facebook) are platform-owned and static.

Prior art concern: Low to moderate. No known prior art on the specific combination of PAIPy™ as namespace replacement with AI-rendered differentiated public presence.

Status: Concept stage. Named and defined. Not yet built.

Identified: May 2026, during Founding Document Part 10 development.

⚑ IP-07 and IP-08 are high priority for Legal Counsel review. File together with IP-03 (AII) as related claims covering the personal AI identity and public presence layer.
IP-08 — HIGH

Auri™ Identity

Concept: "I'm Joe on Auri." A unique, portable, platform-independent personal identity on the Intelligence Superhighway™. A human-readable address that replaces email addresses, domain names, and platform-specific profiles as the universal personal contact and identity point. The identity is governed by the Memory Entity, served through PAIPy™, and resolves to the Member's MQI™ Public Profile for authorized callers.

Novel element: The specific combination of: (1) human-readable AI-network identity that bypasses domain name registration and email provider dependency, (2) PAIPy™ as the resolution protocol replacing DNS, (3) Memory Entity democratic governance of the identity namespace, and (4) the identity resolving to a live, queryable, AI-mediated personal profile.

Prior art: Decentralized identity systems (DID/W3C, ENS/Ethereum Name Service, IndieWeb) address portions of this concept. None combine AI-mediated resolution, democratic governance, and the Intelligence Superhighway™ network architecture.

Prior art concern: Moderate. IP counsel should specifically evaluate the relationship to W3C DID standards to ensure claims do not conflict.

Status: Concept stage. Named and defined. Not yet built. Dependent on PAIPy™ implementation.

Identified: May 2026, during Founding Document Part 10 development.

⚑ IP-07 and IP-08 are high priority for Legal Counsel review. File together with IP-03 (AII). Note: Every Member who introduces themselves as "Joe on Auri" is a distribution event for AuriOS™ — the phrase is designed to prompt "what is Auri?" turning every introduction into an organic marketing moment.
