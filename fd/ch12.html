12.1 The Market Opportunity

The total addressable market is every person using AI tools — currently over 500 million active users globally and growing at a rate that has no historical precedent. ChatGPT reached one hundred million users in sixty days. The AI era is not coming. It is here.

The serviceable addressable market — prosumer and professional users willing to pay for owned, persistent, governed AI memory — is estimated at 50 to 100 million users in the near term. These are the people for whom AI has become a professional tool rather than a curiosity, who have experienced the frustration of rebuilding context in every session, and who understand what it would mean to have an AI that actually knows them.

The serviceable obtainable market in the near term is the early-adopter professional segment — lawyers, doctors, bankers, engineers, developers, executives — with initial focus on the US market where AI adoption is most advanced and the regulatory environment most navigable.

The analogous market is the ISP market of 1994 to 1996. Small technical early adopters in 1994. Mass-market breakout in 18 to 24 months. The people who built ISP infrastructure in 1994 owned the on-ramps when the mainstream arrived. The window is the same. The timing is the same. The people in the room right now are building the on-ramps.

The window is estimated at 18 to 24 months before a major AI lab builds a comparable offering — if they choose to. The governance architecture makes it genuinely difficult for them: it requires ceding control of user data to a non-profit, which is structurally incompatible with their business model.

12.2 Revenue Model

AuriOS™ operates on a membership model — a monthly or annual fee that covers Custos Aurum™ infrastructure access, Auri Identity™, and the full AuriOS™ software stack. The tier structure reflects the depth of capability the Member accesses.

The Entry tier provides basic memory and single-model querying — the right starting point for a new Member building their memory store. The Standard tier adds full memory access and IBT Mode™ — the complete AuriOS™ experience for the professional user. The Premium tier adds agents, priority support, and expanded ingestion capacity — for Members whose use cases require the full system.

Beyond individual membership, the revenue model includes enterprise licensing for corporate AuriOS™ deployment — teams and organizations that want governed AI memory for their people. API access for third-party developers building on MQI™ and PAIPy™. Data portability premium services — enhanced ingestion, legacy data migration, VHS digitization, email archive processing, medical records integration — for Members who want to build their memory store comprehensively and quickly.

The Memory Entity membership fee is separate from the AuriOS™ subscription. It covers Custos Aurum™ infrastructure and governance operations. The revenue sharing model between AuriOS™ as operator and the Memory Entity as infrastructure owner is to be developed with Pete Patterson.

12.3 The Path to Scale

Phase 1 is the Founding Circle — closed, curated, high-engagement, 10 to 25 members. The Founding Circle is not just governance. It is the proof of concept, the first cohort of professional use cases, and the first sales channel.

Phase 2 is invitation expansion — Founding Circle members invite qualified candidates from their professional networks. Each invitation is a warm endorsement from someone who has used the system and believes in it.

Phase 3 is managed public launch — a waitlist, controlled onboarding, capacity management. The goal is not to grow as fast as possible. The goal is to grow with the right Members, in the right order, building the kind of early community that makes the network valuable rather than simply large.

Phase 4 is open enrollment — when the infrastructure, the governance, and the product are ready to serve Members at scale without compromising the quality of the experience.

The network effects compound at every phase. Every Auri Identity™ that exists makes the network more valuable. Every PAIPy™ integration adds a node. Every Member who builds three years of accumulated memory creates a data moat that no competitor can replicate in less than three years.

12.4 The Founding Circle as Go-To-Market

The Founding Circle is simultaneously the governance founding and the go-to-market strategy. Each member represents a specific professional use case and a specific professional network. Each member is both a case study and a distribution channel.

The radiologist use case: medical imaging memory, complete provider history across facilities, appointment preparation that surfaces what the doctor needs to know before the patient arrives. The banker use case: deal history, market intelligence, regulatory compliance memory across engagements. The lawyer use case: case research, precedent memory, client history, document retrieval across matters. The developer use case: code memory, architecture decisions, technical debt tracking across projects.

Each of these use cases is a story. Each story travels through a professional network. Each professional network contains dozens of people who have the same problem and would pay to have it solved. The Founding Circle does not just validate the product. It launches it.

The referral architecture — how Founding Circle members identify, approach, and introduce qualified candidates — will be developed with the members themselves. They know their networks better than anyone. The mechanism needs to be worthy of the relationships.
